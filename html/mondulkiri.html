Mundlkiri

Rolling green hills, thundering waterfalls, and the gentle giants of the jungle.

ABOUT THE PROVINCES

Cambodia's Wild Frontier

Cool, green, and wonderfully empty, Mondulkiri is Cambodia's largest and least populated province. Its name means "meeting of the hills," and you'll understand why the moment you arrive in the small capital of Sen Monorom — surrounded by 360° of rolling pasture, pine forest, and waterfalls thundering through the jungle.


This is the homeland of the indigenous Bunong people, who have lived alongside Asian elephants for centuries. Today, ethical sanctuaries let visitors walk beside retired working elephants in their natural habitat — no riding, no tricks, just respect.

Region: Eastern Cambodia

Capital: Sen Monorom

Elevation: ~800 m (cool nights!)

Currency: Riel / USD

Best time: Nov - Feb

Getting there: 6-7 hr drive from Phnom Penh

Must do: Walk with elephants


Top attractions


1 · Bou Sra Waterfall
Mondulkiri's iconic two-tier waterfall, plunging 30 meters into a jungle gorge.

2 · Elephant Valley Project
An award-winning sanctuary where you observe elephants roaming free in the forest.

3 · Sea Forest Viewpoint
Wave after wave of rolling green hills disappearing into the morning mist.

4 · Bunong Village Homestays
Sleep in a stilted longhouse, share rice wine, and learn ancient forest traditions.

5 · Keo Seima Wildlife Sanctuary
Home to gibbons, sun bears, and one of Asia's last wild Asian elephant populations.
