Southern Cambodia

Kampot

Sleepy riverside town, world-famous pepper, and a misty mountain in the clouds.

ABOUT THE PROVINCES

Where Cambodia Slows Down

Tucked between Bokor Mountain and the Praek Tuek Chhu River, Kampot is the kind of place travelers come for one night and stay for a week. Colonial shophouses line the riverfront, fishing boats drift past at sunset, and the surrounding countryside is patterned with the famous Kampot pepper plantations a spice once reserved for European royalty.


A short drive away you'll find the abandoned hill station of Bokor, the salt fields of Kep, and the legendary Kep Crab Market, where blue swimmer crabs are stir-fried in green Kampot pepper sauce within minutes of being caught.

Region: Southern coast

Population: ~600,000

Best time: Dec - Apr

Currency: Riel / USD

Getting there: 3 hr from Phnom Penh by bus

Famous for: Pepper, salt, durian

Must try: Kep Pepper Crab


Top attractions


1 · Bokor National Park
An eerie 1920s French hill station shrouded in mist, with sweeping ocean views.

2 · Kampot Pepper Farms
our family-run organic farms producing the world's most aromatic pepper.

3 · Kep Crab Market
Famous seafood village just 25 km away order pepper crab with a cold coconut.

4 · Praek Tuek Chhu River
Sunset cruises, kayaking, and firefly tours beneath the mangroves.

5 · Salt Fields & Caves
Photogenic salt pans by day, prehistoric cave temples like Phnom Chhngok by morning.
