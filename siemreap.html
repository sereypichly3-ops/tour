Siem Reap

Gateway to Angkor where ancient stone gods still watch over the jungle.

ABOUT THE PROVINCES

A City Built Around a Wonder

Siem Reap, whose name means "the defeat of Siam," is the cultural capital of Cambodia and the launching pad for the Angkor Archaeological Park — a UNESCO World Heritage site spread across more than 400 square kilometers. The city itself is small and walkable, blending French colonial architecture, vibrant night markets, world-class restaurants, and a booming arts scene.


Whether you wake before dawn to watch the sun rise behind the five towers of Angkor Wat, lose yourself in the smiling stone faces of Bayon, or drift across the floating villages of Tonle Sap, Siem Reap rewards every kind of traveler.

Region: Northwest Cambodia

Population: ~1.0 million

Best time: Nov - Mar (cool & dry)

Currency: Riel / USD

Airport: SAI (Siem Reap-Angkor Int'l)

Must try: Khmer Red Curry, Lok Lak


Top attractions


1 · Angkor Wat
The largest religious monument in the world. Best at sunrise from the western reflecting pool.

2 · Bayon Temple
216 serene stone faces carved into 54 towers — the spiritual heart of Angkor Thom.

3 · Ta Prohm
The "Tomb Raider" temple, slowly being reclaimed by giant silk-cotton tree roots.

4 · Tonle Sap Lake
Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake, home to floating Khmer and Vietnamese villages.

5 · Pub Street & Old Market
A buzzing pedestrian strip of cafes, bars, street food, and crafts.
